STARTERS

Corn cheese balls: Corn cheese balls are a delightful snack made by mixing boiled corn kernels, grated cheese, mashed potatoes, and spices. Form the mixture into balls, coat them with breadcrumbs, and deep-fry until golden brown. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Gobi Manchurian:Marinate cauliflower florets in a mixture of flour, cornstarch, and spices, then deep-fry until crispy. Sauté ginger, garlic, and green chilies in a pan, add soy sauce, ketchup, and fried cauliflower, and toss until coated. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot.

Paneer-pakora Paneer pakora is made by dipping paneer cubes in a spiced gram flour (besan) batter. Deep-fry the battered paneer until golden and crispy. Serve hot with chutney or ketchup.

Aloo Gobi:Paneer pakora is made by dipping paneer cubes in a spiced gram flour (besan) batter. Deep-fry the battered paneer until golden and crispy. Serve hot with chutney or ketchup.

Medu Vada: Medu vada is made by grinding urad dal into a smooth batter, mixing it with spices and herbs, and shaping it into doughnut-like rings. Deep-fry the rings until golden brown and crispy, then serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Paneer 65: Combine flour, cocoa powder, sugar, buttermilk, and eggs. Add red food coloring, then bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30-35 minutes.

Pani Poori:Pani poori is made by filling crispy hollow puris with a mixture of spicy tamarind water, chickpeas, potatoes, and chutney. Serve immediately to enjoy the burst of flavors and textures in each bite.

Soup: Sauté chopped onions, garlic, and mixed vegetables in a pot until softened. Add vegetable broth, season with salt, pepper, and herbs, and simmer until vegetables are tender. Blend partially for a creamy texture or serve as is.

Fries: Cut potatoes into thin strips, soak in cold water, then pat dry. Deep-fry in hot oil until golden and crispy, then drain on paper towels. Season with salt and serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
